Last updated: 2025-09-05

Trading digital assets and using algorithmic strategies involves substantial risk. You should carefully consider whether such trading is suitable for you in light of your financial condition and risk tolerance.

1. Market risk and volatility

Digital asset markets are highly volatile. Prices can move rapidly and unpredictably. You may lose some or all of your invested capital.

2. Liquidity and slippage

Executing orders—especially large orders—may incur slippage. Certain assets or venues may be illiquid, causing spreads to widen or orders to remain unfilled.

3. Technology, smart contract, and cybersecurity risk

Trading systems, smart contracts, APIs, and networks can fail or be attacked. Bugs, outages, MEV, front-running, chain reorgs, and oracle failures can result in losses.

4. Counterparty and venue risk

Use of centralized exchanges, brokers, and other third parties carries insolvency, operational, and legal risks. DeFi venues carry protocol and governance risks.

5. Regulatory and tax risk

Regulatory frameworks for digital assets are evolving. Changes in law or interpretation may impact trading, liquidity, or availability. You are responsible for your tax obligations.

6. Non-custodial model

QuantsEdge is non-custodial. You control your wallets, keys, and exchange accounts. You are responsible for safeguarding credentials and implementing appropriate security measures.

8. Suitability

Digital asset trading may not be suitable for all investors. Consider your objectives, experience, and risk appetite, and consult independent advisors where appropriate.
